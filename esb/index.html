PHASE 01 • AFT
💪

Army Fitness Test

Mandatory Prerequisite • Min: 80 pts per Event • Max: 500 pts • 70 min

1. 3-Rep Max Deadlift (MDL)

Hex bar 60 lbs. 3 continuous repetitions. Max 2 attempts. Total-body strength: posterior chain, glutes, core.

2. Hand-Release Push-Up (HRP)

Push-up with T-release (arms extended out). Max reps in 2 min. Body aligned — chest, hips, and thighs touch the ground.

3. Sprint-Drag-Carry (SDC)

5 legs x 50m = 250m total. Sprint → Drag sled 90 lbs → Lateral shuffle → Carry 2x40 lbs kettlebells → Final sprint.

4. Plank (PLK)

Isometric plank (forearms + toes). Max time until failure. 1 verbal warning before the event ends.

5. 2-Mile Run (2MR)

3.2 km run on a track or paved surface. Walking allowed. Final event — pacing and strategy are critical.

PHASE 02 • EQUIPMENT
🧭 LAND NAV

Lensatic Compass

Compass M2 • Components and Use

Components

  1. Cover — holds the sighting wire for alignment
  2. Bezel (rotating ring) — 3° clicks, luminous markings
  3. Floating Dial — floats freely, points to magnetic North
  4. Lens (rear sight) — magnifies the reading, sighting slot
  5. Thumb Loop — secures on the thumb for stability
  6. Straightedge — cm/inch scale on the bottom edge

Procedure: Shoot an Azimuth

  1. Open the compass — cover 90°, lens holder ~45°. Position the thumb loop on your thumb.
  2. Rotate the bezel — align the desired azimuth with the black index line on the body.
  3. Aim at the target — cover's sighting wire aligned with the lens's sighting slot.
  4. Read the azimuth — through the lens, read the value on the floating dial aligned with the index line.

Day Navigation

Sighting wire + slot. Precision of ~1°. Dominant eye on the lens.

Night Navigation

Tritium markers on the bezel and floating dial. Align the luminous points.

PHASE 02 • METHOD
🧭 LAND NAV

Azimuth and Protractor

Coordinate Plotting • Grid-Magnetic Conversion

1. Plot a Coordinate (6-digit)

0453 1663 = grid 04 53 • 16 63

Protractor on line 04. Measure 530m to the right on the 1:25k scale.
Protractor on line 16. Measure 630m up. Mark the point.

Remember: RIGHT AND UP.

2. Measure Azimuth on the Map (Grid)

Connect points A and B with a straight line. Align the protractor's center on A. Align the protractor's base line with the nearest vertical grid line. Read the angle on the scale.

Grid Azimuth — angle measured on the map using the grid lines (GN).

3. Convert to Magnetic Azimuth

LARS Rule:
Left Add — if MN is to the left of GN, add the G-M Angle
Right Subtract — if MN is to the right of GN, subtract

Example: MN 6° West (W) of GN. MN is to the LEFT. Add!
Grid AZ 68° + 6° = Magnetic AZ 74°

Plotting

Identify the 1000m square by the first 2 digits. Measure the last 3 digits in meters on the correct protractor scale. Always left to right, then bottom to top.

8-Digit Grid

For 10m precision. Ex: 0453 1663. The 2 extra digits divide the square into 100 parts. Identical reading but with a finer scale.

Common Error

Forgetting to convert Grid AZ to Magnetic AZ — an error of several degrees that can cost the checkpoint. Always check the G-M Angle in the map margin.

Back Azimuth

Return azimuth. If AZ < 180°: add 180. If AZ > 180°: subtract 180. Ex: AZ 74° → back AZ 254°. Used to confirm position.

PHASE 03 • DETAIL
🔫 WEAPONS

M4 Carbine / M16A4

Field Strip and Functions Check

📦 Disassembly (Field Strip)

  • Clear the weapon — magazine out, bolt to rear, inspect chamber
  • Push takedown pin (front) — right to left
  • Pivot upper receiver open
  • Pull charging handle + bolt carrier group (BCG) to rear
  • Remove charging handle from BCG
  • Remove firing pin retaining pin, firing pin, bolt cam pin, bolt
  • Push pivot pin (rear) — separate upper/lower
  • Remove buffer + buffer spring

🔧 Reassembly

  • Reverse of disassembly
  • Bolt cam pin installed in the correct orientation
  • Firing pin retaining pin fully seated
  • Charging handle properly seated in the BCG
  • BCG slides freely in the upper receiver
  • Takedown and pivot pins fully seated
  • Buffer and spring correctly installed

✅ Functions Check (8 Steps)

  1. Clear the weapon. No magazine. Confirm empty chamber visually and by touch.
  2. SAFE. Selector on SAFE. Pull the trigger. Hammer must NOT fall. FAIL = defective safety selector
  3. SEMI. Selector on SEMI. Pull the trigger and hold it back. Hammer falls.
  4. SEMI reset. Hold the trigger back. Pull the charging handle. Release the trigger. Hear a click. Pull the trigger. Hammer falls. FAIL = defective disconnector
  5. BURST/AUTO. Selector on BURST/AUTO. Pull the charging handle. Pull the trigger and hold. Hammer falls.
  6. Cycling. Hold the trigger. Pull the charging handle 3x (BURST) or 1x (AUTO) and release. Release the trigger. Pull. Hammer falls.
  7. SEMI. Confirm semi-automatic function again.
  8. SAFE. Selector on SAFE. Weapon safe. COMPLETE
PHASE 03 • DETAIL
🔫 WEAPONS

M17 / M18 Pistol

Field Strip and Functions Check

📦 Disassembly (Field Strip)

  • Clear the weapon — magazine out, slide to rear, inspect chamber (visual + tactile)
  • Rotate takedown lever (left side) 90° downward
  • Slide forward, remove from the frame
  • Remove the recoil spring assembly
  • Remove the barrel from the slide
  • No further disassembly of the fire control group is needed

🔧 Reassembly

  • Insert the barrel into the slide
  • Insert the recoil spring assembly (smaller spring end toward the barrel)
  • Slide + barrel + spring onto the frame, align the rails
  • Slide fully to the rear, rotate the takedown lever 90° upward
  • Functions check mandatory

✅ Functions Check (4 Steps)

  1. Clear the weapon. Magazine out. Eject the round from the chamber. Lock the slide to the rear. Inspect visually + tactile check. ALWAYS CLEAR
  2. Slide Lock. Press the slide catch. Slide moves forward with force. Hammer remains cocked. FAIL = weak recoil spring
  3. Trigger Function. Pull the trigger. Striker fires. Hold the trigger, pull the slide, release the trigger. Reset click. Pull again. Striker fires. FAIL = trigger bar/sear
  4. Safety Function. Safety on SAFE. Pull the trigger. Does NOT fire. Safety on FIRE. Pull. Fires. COMPLETE
PHASE 03 • DETAIL
🔫 WEAPONS

M240B • M249 • M2 .50 Cal

Checking Procedures

M240B (7.62mm)

Headspace & Timing: Set at the factory — fixed barrel, no field adjustment required. Just verify the barrel is fully locked (locking lever).

Functions Check: Bolt to the rear, trigger — bolt moves forward. Hold the trigger, bolt stays forward. Release, bolt remains. Load the belt, charging handle to the rear 2x, trigger — fires. Hold = continuous fire. Release = ceases.

M249 SAW (5.56mm)

Field Strip: Remove barrel, handguard, operating rod + spring, bolt assembly, trigger housing.

Functions Check: Bolt closed. Load belt/drum. Pull the charging handle to the rear and release. Trigger — bolt moves forward, fires. AUTO and SEMI positions tested. Feed pawls engage correctly.

M2 .50 Cal (12.7mm)

Headspace: Use the headspace & timing gauge (GO/NO-GO). Barrel fully threaded, back off 2 clicks. GO gauge = fits between bolt face and barrel. NO-GO gauge = does NOT fit.

Timing: Adjust the timing nut until the shot fires at the correct point in the cycle. FIRE gauge = .020" gap between bolt face and barrel. Verify manual firing with the charging handle.

AT4 / M67 / Claymore

AT4 (84mm): Confirm 90m backblast area (60° cone). Remove safeties. Extend the tube. Arm the firing mechanism. Pop-up sight. Fire: squeeze the forward trigger.

M67: Remove the safety clip + pin. Firm grip. Throw in an arc. 5m kill radius / 15m casualty radius. Seek cover after the throw.

M18A1 Claymore: Aiming stakes. M57 clacker — 3 squeezes (test, arm, fire). Backblast safe area: 16m lateral, 100m frontal.

Medical Lane

Tactical Combat Casualty Care • MARCH Protocol

M — Massive Hemorrhage

Combat Application Tourniquet (CAT) applied high and tight. 2 tourniquets if needed. Junctional TQ for groin/axilla. Hemostatic gauze (Celox/QuikClot) with direct pressure for 3 min.

A — Airway

Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) 28F. Measure: tip of the nose to the earlobe. Lubricate. Insert with the bevel facing the septum. Emergency cricothyrotomy if needed. Recovery position.

R — Respiration

Check for symmetric chest rise. Chest seal (Bolin/Hyfin) on open torso wounds. Needle Decompression (NDC) 14G, 3.25" — 2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicular line. 90° angle.

C — Circulation

Assess radial pulse (present = SBP ≥ 80 mmHg). IV/IO access if needed. Fluid resuscitation: Hextend or LR. Prevent hypothermia: blankets, blizzard blanket, passive rewarming.

H — Hypothermia / Head Injury

Prevent heat loss. Thermal blanket (blizzard). Chemical heat packs at the axilla and groin. Assess pupils, Glasgow Coma Scale. Note changes in consciousness.

Evacuation / CASEVAC

Drags: Ranger roll, collar, two-man. Carries: fireman, pack strap. Litter carries: 4-man. Prepare DD 1380. Communicate priority to command.

Patrol Lane

Patrol and Combat Operations

React to Contact

Direct contact: "CONTACT FRONT/LEFT/RIGHT/REAR". Seek immediate cover. Return fire toward the enemy. Squad leader maneuvers the flanking element. Suppress + assault.

React to IED

5 C's: Confirm (is it an IED?), Clear (the area of civilians), Cordon (300m perimeter), Check (visual inspection), Control (report via radio to EOD). Never touch or move the device.

React to Ambush

Near ambush (inside the kill zone): immediate aggressive assault toward the ambush. Far ambush (outside the kill zone): return fire + seek cover + maneuver against it.

Hand & Arm Signals

Standardized signals: Rally Point, Halt, Enemy Spotted, Wedge Formation, Increase Speed, Head Count. Silent visual communication. Confirmation: point + signal + execute.

Movement Techniques

Traveling: low threat, max speed. Traveling Overwatch: moderate threat, overwatch to the front. Bounding Overwatch: high threat, alternates cover/movement. Formations: wedge, file, line.

Call for Fire / Reporting

Grid/Polar/Shift from known point. 6 elements: observer ID, warning order, target location, target description, method of engagement, method of fire & control. Spot Report: who, where, what.

Communications Lane

Tactical Communications • Overview

SINCGARS Radio (RT-1523)

Basic operation: power on, load single channel freq (SC), FH mode sync, fill COMSEC (KY-57/100). Time sync via GPS (PLGR/DAGR). Troubleshooting: check battery, antenna, cable connection, fill.

9-Line MEDEVAC

Medical evacuation request — standardized NATO format. 9 lines of critical information. See the next slide for full details and a real example.

Call for Fire

Request for indirect fire support: artillery, mortars, naval gunfire. Grid method (most common), polar, shift from a known point. Fire adjustment: OT direction, correction in meters.

SALUTE Report

Intelligence: Size, Activity, Location (grid), Unit (uniform/insignia), Time (DTG), Equipment. Immediate report of observed enemy activity.

LACE Report

Logistics status: Liquid (water), Ammo, Casualties, Equipment (status). Used for combat SITREPs.

Radio Discipline

Pro-words: OVER, OUT, ROGER, WILCO, BREAK, I SAY AGAIN, AUTHENTICATE. Phonetic alphabet. Numbers: "niner", "tree", "fife". Jammed: switch to FH or an alternate frequency.

9-Line MEDEVAC

Medical Evacuation Request • Explanation and Example

Meaning

Line 1Pickup Location (Grid) — 6- or 8-digit MGRS coordinate of the landing point.
Line 2Radio Freq, Call Sign, Suffix — Frequency and identification over the air.
Line 3Patients by Precedence — A-URGENT B-PRIORITY C-ROUTINE D-CONV.
Line 4Special Equipment — A=None, B=Hoist, C=Extraction, D=Ventilator.
Line 5Patients by Type — L=Litter + A=Ambulatory. Ex: L+2, A+0.
Line 6Security at Pickup Site — N=None, P=Possible, E=Enemy, X=Escort.
Line 7Marking Method — A=Panels, B=Pyro, C=Smoke, D=None, E=IR Strobe.
Line 8Patient Nationality — A=US Mil, B=US Civ, C=Non-US, D=Non-US Civ, E=EPW.
Line 9NBC Contamination — N=Nuclear, B=Biological, C=Chemical. Peacetime: NONE.

CBRN Lane

Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear Defense

MOPP Levels

MOPP 0: Only mask carried. MOPP 1: JSLIST worn. MOPP 2: + Overboots. MOPP 3: + Mask & hood. MOPP 4: + Gloves (all sealed).

M50 Mask Don/Doff

DON (<9s): Hold your breath, remove headgear, pull the mask over your face, tighten straps (bottom-top-front), exhale hard to seal. DOFF: no contamination, reverse procedure.

MOPP Gear Exchange

Exchanging contaminated JSLIST in a CBRN environment. Buddy team. Cut or remove without contaminating the skin. M291 SDK for skin decon. M295 wipes for equipment.

Detection Kits

M8 Paper: Detects G (Nerve) — yellow, V (Nerve) — green, H (Blister/mustard) — red. M9 Paper: Detects liquid agents (turns pink/red). M256A1: Portable chemical detector kit with ampoules.

12-Mile Ruck March

12-Mile March • Final Event

19.3 km
12 Miles
3:00:00
Max Time
35 lbs
Min Weight

Forced march of 19.3 km with a rucksack weighing at least 35 pounds (~16 kg) in up to 3 hours. Minimum pace: 15 minutes per mile (9:20/km). Combat boots (AR 670-1) mandatory. No outside assistance. Hydration at checkpoints. Dry rucksack weight (no water, no consumed MREs, no M4). Concludes all ESB testing.

Preparation

Hydrate well 48h before. Complex carbohydrates the night before. Anti-chafing (Body Glide, Leukotape). Test the rucksack and adjust straps.

Strategy

Steady pace: ~14:30/mi in the first third, hold it through the middle 10 km, push the last 3 km. Short, regular strides. Don't stop at checkpoints.

Weigh-In

Rucksack weighed BEFORE and AFTER the march. Minimum dry weight: 35 lbs. Standard items: full camelback, extra uniform, MRE, poncho, cold weather gear. No "fake" weights (anvils, dumbbells).
